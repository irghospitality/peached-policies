Employee Meals

We encourage employees to eat before arriving for work whenever possible so they are prepared to begin their shift and provide excellent service from the start.

We understand this is not always possible. When employees need to eat during their shift, the following guidelines apply:

Ordering Food
  • Employee meals should be ordered within the first 30 minutes after service begins, unless directed otherwise by management.
  • During periods of high business volume or an unexpected rush, management may require meal orders to be delayed until business allows.
  • Employee meals should consist of menu items that are quick to prepare and easy to eat.
Eating During Your Shift
  • Employees may eat only when approved by management and when doing so will not negatively impact operations or guest service.
  • Employees should eat in designated employee areas whenever possible.
  • Employees who are actively serving guests or responsible for open tables may not eat while those responsibilities are ongoing.
  • Guest service always takes priority over employee meals.
Staff Food

From time to time, approved food (such as overproduced food or "dead" food) may be made available for employees. When this occurs:

  • Food will generally be boxed for employees.
  • Employees should wait until after the service period has ended to eat unless otherwise approved by management.
  • Employees should not interrupt service to eat available food.
These guidelines are intended to support employee well-being while ensuring we continue to provide exceptional hospitality and maintain smooth restaurant operations.
