Breaks

We believe employees perform their best when they have the opportunity to rest and recharge. Because restaurant operations are guest-driven, break timing may vary based on business needs, staffing levels, and guest volume. Employees are expected to work with management to ensure breaks are taken in a manner that supports both exceptional guest service and the team.

Hourly Employees

Unpaid 30-Minute Breaks

Hourly employees scheduled to work six (6) or more consecutive hours will generally receive one 30-minute unpaid break.

Break Expectations
  • Employees must clock out before beginning their break and clock back in before returning to work.
  • Breaks will generally occur after approximately six hours of work but may be delayed if the restaurant is in an active service period, experiencing a rush, or operational needs require employees to remain on the floor.
  • Employees may not perform any work while clocked out. If work is performed during a break for any reason, notify a manager immediately so your time can be properly recorded.
  • Employees are expected to return from their break on time.

Paid Rest Breaks

Paid rest breaks of up to 15 minutes may be provided during longer shifts at the discretion of management based on staffing levels and business needs.

Rest Break Expectations
  • Rest breaks are paid working time.
  • Rest breaks are intended as brief opportunities to rest.
  • Rest breaks may not be combined with unpaid breaks or used to arrive late or leave early.
  • Rest breaks must be approved by management and taken in designated areas.
  • Smoking or vaping is permitted only during an approved break and only in designated smoking areas.

Managers

Managers have greater flexibility in when they take breaks. They are expected to exercise sound judgment while balancing their leadership responsibilities and should take their break at the time that makes the most operational sense.

Before leaving the building, managers must ensure:

  • Opening or shift preparation responsibilities have been completed.
  • Their department and team are fully staffed, organized, and prepared for service.
  • There is adequate management coverage.
  • The General Manager or acting Manager on Duty has been informed they are leaving, approximately how long they expect to be gone, and how they can be reached if needed.
Communication is critical. Managers should never leave the building without notifying another member of management.

During Service

Providing exceptional hospitality to our guests is our top priority. For that reason:

  • Breaks will generally not be taken during active service rushes.
  • Managers may delay breaks when necessary to maintain guest service and operational efficiency.
  • Whenever possible, breaks should be taken before or after peak service periods.
  • Only one employee from the same department or work station should be on break at a time unless approved by management.

General Expectations

All employees are expected to:

  • Follow management's direction regarding break timing.
  • Clock in and out accurately.
  • Return promptly from breaks.
  • Help ensure their responsibilities are covered before leaving for a break.
  • Notify a manager if they were unable to receive their unpaid 30-minute break due to operational demands.
