Uniform Policy

Our appearance is part of the guest experience. A clean, professional, and consistent look helps create a welcoming atmosphere, builds trust with our guests, and reflects the pride we take in our work. Employees are expected to arrive in uniform, well-groomed, and ready for every shift.

If you have questions about an item or need an accommodation, speak with a manager before your shift.

Hosts

Hosts are often the first and last team members our guests interact with. Their appearance sets the tone for the dining experience and should reflect the warm, polished, and welcoming hospitality of The Peached Tortilla.

Tops

  • Professional blouses, button-down shirts, polos, cardigans, or other business-casual tops are permitted
  • Tasteful colors and patterns are welcome
  • No graphic prints, promotional branding, or large visible logos
  • Clothing must be clean, wrinkle-free, and in good condition

Bottoms

  • Jeans, dress pants, chinos, tailored pants, dresses, skirts, or shorts are permitted
  • Dresses and skirts must be an appropriate length for a professional hospitality environment
  • Shorts must have at least a 5-inch inseam and fully cover the backside while standing or bending
  • Clothing should fit properly and may not be excessively baggy or overly tight
  • No leggings, joggers, athletic wear, cargo pants, or distressed, torn, ripped, or frayed clothing

Servers, Barbacks & Food Runners

Our server uniform is not just a T-Shirt, it is part of the brand's identity. A consistent appearance across the team builds trust, elevates professionalism, and makes every staff member look like part of a coordinated team.

Tops

  • Gray Peached Tortilla t-shirts only
  • Shirts must be clean, wrinkle-free, and in good condition

Bottoms

  • Black or blue denim pants or shorts only — no leggings, joggers, athletic wear, cargo pants, other pant styles, or skirts
  • Clothing should fit properly and may not be excessively baggy or overly tight
  • Shorts must have at least a 5-inch inseam and fully cover the backside while standing or bending
  • No faded, distressed, torn, ripped, or frayed denim

Bartenders

Bartenders play a key role in shaping the guest experience. They create an inviting atmosphere by engaging with guests, delivering exceptional service, and keeping the energy of the bar flowing. Their appearance should reflect the same level of professionalism and hospitality they bring to every interaction.

Tops

  • Must have a collar, either button-down or polo style
  • Short or long sleeves only; sleeveless shirts are not allowed
  • Solid colors are preferred; small stripes or subtle business-casual patterns are allowed
  • No large graphics, images, wording, or visible logos
  • Shirts must be clean, pressed, wrinkle-free, and in good condition

Bottoms

  • Pants and shorts must be solid dark colors: black, dark grey, dark blue, or dark denim
  • Styles may include jeans, chinos, or dress pants. No skirts.
  • Clothing should fit properly and may not be excessively baggy or overly tight
  • Shorts must have at least a 5-inch inseam and fully cover the backside when standing or bending
  • No frays, rips, holes, fading, or distressing

Manager on Duty (MOD)

Managers are expected to present themselves in a manner that reflects their leadership position. Attire must be clean, neat, well-maintained, and appropriate for interacting with guests, employees, and vendors.

Managers are expected to set the standard for their team through their appearance. Regardless of assignment — including while working an Expo/MOD shift — managers are expected to remain professionally dressed and guest-ready. T-shirts, athletic wear, clothing with large logos or graphics, and other excessively casual attire are not permitted.

Shoes

  • Closed-toe, closed-heel, slip-resistant shoes are required
  • No brightly colored shoes
  • Solid color sneakers, walking shoes, flats, or boots are permitted
  • No high heels, flip-flops, slippers, or open-toed shoes
  • Shoes must be cleaned before each shift and kept in good repair

Additional Requirements

  • Bartenders and Servers must wear aprons at all times while working. Aprons must be clean, wrinkle-free, and in good condition.
  • No hats, sunglasses, or bandanas are allowed while working unless approved for medical or religious reasons.

Hygiene & Grooming

  • Hair must be clean, neat, and pulled back if shoulder-length or longer
  • Facial hair must be neatly trimmed
  • No hats or head coverings unless approved for medical or religious reasons
  • Jewelry must be minimal and must not interfere with job duties
  • Hands and nails must be clean; nail polish, if worn, must be chip-free
  • Clothing must be clean, stain-free, and odor-free at the start of the shift
  • Perfume, cologne, and scented lotions should be used lightly or avoided, as some people are sensitive to strong fragrance
Uniform & Property Agreement

By working in a uniformed position, you understand and agree to the following:

  • The two issued Peached Tortilla t-shirts are yours to keep. Additional shirts may be purchased at the employee discount rate.
  • Aprons are company property and must be returned upon resignation, termination, or separation from employment.
  • You are responsible for laundering, ironing, and generally caring for all issued uniform items.
  • Uniform items may not be altered.
  • If an apron is not returned or is damaged through negligence, the Company may deduct the replacement cost from your final paycheck where permitted by law.
